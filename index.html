Saint Teresa of Ávila

The woman that became the first female Doctor of the Church.

A short biography of St Teresa of Avila

  • 1515 - St Teresa (Teresa de Cepeda y Ahumada) was born in Avila, Spain on 28th March
  • 1520 - When St. Teresa was about five years old, she and her older brother snuck into Moorish territory, hoping to become martyrs for Christ. An uncle brought them back home.
  • 1531 - Her wealthy, strict, and religious father sent her to a convent school when she was 16.
  • 1562 - St. Teresa founded the convent of St. Joseph, with the blessing of Pope Paul IV.
  • 1565 - Under the direction of her confessors, Teresa wrote about some of these experiences in an autobiography.
  • 1566 - The Way of Perfection was published. Teresa called this a "living book" and in it set out to teach her nuns how to progress through prayer and Christian meditation.
  • 1582 - St. Teresa of Avila died at 67 years old on October 4th.
  • 1588 - The Interior Castle, or The Mansions, was written in 1577, and published in 1588. It contained the basis for what she felt should be the ideal journey of faith.
  • 1622 - St. Teresa was canonized (declared a Roman Catholic church saint) by Pope Gregory XV on March 12th.
  • 1970 - Pope St. Paul VI proclaimed St. Teresa as one of the first two woman Doctors of the Church, along with 14th century Dominican St. Catherine of Siena.

“In Spain, Teresa of Avila offered to the world something profoundly mystical. Her mystical experience is the most successful culmination of the divine marriage between the aspiring soul and the liberating Christ, and it is here that man’s helpless crying will and God’s omnipotent all-fulfilling Will embrace each other.”

-- Contemporary spiritual master Sri Chinmoy
